Prarabdham — Your Chart Report
What You Are Holding

This report is not a prediction of your future. It is a map — a precise snapshot of the karma a soul carries into this life, drawn from the positions of the planets at the exact moment and place of your birth.

To understand what this map actually is, it helps to understand the worldview within which it was created.

The tradition of Jyotish — Vedic astrology — does not begin with planets. It begins with a fundamental understanding of existence itself. Everything that exists, from the smallest particle to the largest galaxy, is not merely connected to one consciousness — it is made of it, moves within it, and returns to it. The separation we experience in daily life is the experience of the journey, not the reality of what we are.

In the Vedic understanding, this consciousness — pure, infinite, complete in itself — moved within itself by its own will. That movement is creation. Not something separate from consciousness, but consciousness differentiating within itself — becoming space, time, energy, matter, and all the forms we experience as the universe. The planets, the stars, the earth, and every being on it are not separate from this process. They are this process, at different scales, at different stages of the same journey.

Dharma is the intelligent principle sustaining the movement of all existence toward its source. Not a rulebook imposed from outside, but the very nature of how consciousness moves — like a river whose nature is to flow, to sustain everything along its banks, to carve through whatever resistance it meets, and to finally dissolve into the ocean it came from. This sustaining intelligence ensures that nothing — no soul, no experience, no friction — is wasted on the journey toward the source.

Karma is the movement of this Dharma through individual experience. Every tendency, every pattern, every recurring experience in a life is part of the soul's specific journey within this larger movement. Not punishment, not reward — but the precise friction needed for this particular consciousness to move toward liberation.

Liberation — Moksha — is not escape from existence. It is the soul returning to its original nature. The state of the absolute and the state of a liberated consciousness are not two different states. The bliss of liberation is not new bliss that arrived — it is the bliss of the source, always present beneath every layer of karma and experience.

Your birth chart is a map of where your soul is on this journey — at this moment, in this life, with these specific conditions.

Why Knowing Your Prarabdha Matters

Most people come to astrology looking for answers about what will happen. These are natural human questions. But they rest on an assumption — that life is something happening to us, and we are trying to navigate it from outside.

Jyotish begins from a different place entirely.

If the soul is a unit of consciousness on a journey — carrying specific karma, moving through specific conditions, with a direction that points toward liberation — then the question is not what will happen. The question is who am I, what am I carrying, and what is this life asking me to work through.

Without this clarity, a person experiences their karma as random — good luck and bad luck, reward and punishment, unfair burdens and unearned gifts. They spend their energy reacting. Resisting what is difficult. Clinging to what is comfortable. Neither reaction moves them forward.

With this clarity, the same experiences are seen differently. A persistent difficulty is not a sign of being cursed or unlucky. It is a specific condition the soul is working through. A recurring pattern in relationships, in work, in health — is not random misfortune. It is the same karma presenting itself again, asking to be understood rather than escaped.

This shift — from reacting to understanding — is the beginning of conscious participation in one's own life. And conscious participation is the beginning of genuine inner work.

Astrology does not give you liberation. No external system can. What it gives you is an honest map of where you are — what you are carrying, what you are avoiding, what you are ready for. What you do with that map is entirely your own work.

How to Use This Report

This report works on four levels. Each level builds on the one before it.

The first level — self understanding. Before asking what will happen, ask what is already present. What tendencies did you bring into this life. Where does your energy naturally flow. Where does it get blocked. What patterns keep repeating. Your chart answers these questions with remarkable honesty if approached without fear or expectation.

The second level — timing. The Dasha system shows the quality of different periods of your life — not what will happen but what kind of energy is operating. Knowing this, you can work with each period rather than against it. A Saturn period does not mean suffering. It means a period where discipline, responsibility, and confronting what is real are the dominant themes.

The third level — relationships. Not to judge compatibility as pass or fail — but to understand what two people bring to each other, where natural harmony exists, and where conscious effort is needed.

The fourth and most important level — sadhana. Understanding what you are carrying in this life, what your specific frictions are, what your natural tendencies pull you toward and away from — this knowledge, taken seriously, becomes the foundation of genuine inner work.

One honest caution before you read further — astrology is a tool for the conscious. Used unconsciously it becomes either a source of anxiety or a crutch. A person who reads their chart and concludes "my Saturn is weak so I will always struggle" has used the map to stop walking. A person who reads the same chart and understands "my Saturn asks me to develop discipline and face what is real" has used the map to walk more purposefully. The chart is the same. The difference is the quality of attention brought to it.

What This Report Can Tell You
  • The nature and tendencies you were born with
  • The specific karmic patterns active in this life
  • The quality and direction of different periods of your life
  • The areas of life that carry the most friction for your growth
  • The natural strengths available to you
  • The timing of significant shifts in life conditions
  • The sadhana direction most aligned with your specific chart conditions
What This Report Cannot Tell You
  • Exactly what will happen and when — the chart shows tendencies, not certainties
  • What choices you will make — free will operates within karma, not outside it
  • Whether you will pursue liberation or ignore it — that is always your choice
  • Anything with certainty if your birth time is inaccurate — the chart is only as reliable as the data it is built on
How to Approach What You Are About to Read

Do not hold anything here too lightly. In difficult times, what seems philosophical or abstract often becomes the understanding that carries you through. The insight you skim past today may be exactly what you need later.

And equally — do not hold anything too tightly. Going too deep into any single interpretation, any single fear, any single prediction creates its own obstruction. The chart is a map, not a verdict. Hold it with enough seriousness to learn from it and enough lightness to remain free.

A Prarabdha is a released arrow. It cannot be called back. What you can do — through honest self understanding, through genuine inner work, through conscious engagement with your own karma — is develop enough inner clarity to observe where the arrow lands, understand what it means, and respond rather than react. That is the purpose of this report. That is the purpose of Prarabdham.
