Glossary

This glossary covers the key terms used across Prarabdham. It is not a comprehensive dictionary of Jyotish — it is a practical reference for understanding what you encounter in your chart reading and across this site.
Ashtakavarga
A system of evaluating planetary strength based on points contributed by each planet and the Lagna to every house. Used to assess the strength of transits and the overall support available in different areas of life.
Ashtadasha Varga — Shodashavarga
The sixteen divisional charts of Jyotish. Each chart divides the Rasi into a specific number of equal parts to examine a specific area of life in greater detail than the main chart alone can show.
Antardasha
The sub period within a Maha Dasha. Each Maha Dasha contains nine Antardashas ruled by each planet in sequence.
Atmakaraka
The planet that has traveled the highest degree in the birth chart. In Jaimini astrology it represents the soul's primary intention and deepest lesson in this life.
Ayanamsa
The difference in degrees between the tropical zodiac used in Western astrology and the sidereal zodiac used in Vedic astrology. Lahiri ayanamsa is the most widely used standard in Indian Jyotish.
Bhava
House. The twelve divisions of the birth chart each representing a specific area of life experience.
Bhinnastakavarga
The individual Ashtakavarga table for each planet — showing the specific point distribution of one planet across all twelve houses.
Chara Karaka
In Jaimini astrology, the seven or eight significators determined by planetary degrees in the birth chart.
Combust — Asta
When a planet comes within a specific degree range of the Sun it is considered combust. Its independent expression becomes absorbed into the Sun's energy.
Dasha
A planetary time period system. Vimshottari Dasha is the most widely used system in Jyotish — spanning 120 years across nine planetary periods beginning from the Moon's Nakshatra at birth.
Debilitation — Neecha
The sign in which a planet's energy is most challenged — where its natural expression meets the most friction. Not a curse or permanent weakness but a specific karmic condition asking for conscious development.
Dig Bala
Directional strength. Each planet has a specific house where it gains maximum directional strength.
Divisional Chart — Varga
A chart derived from the birth chart by dividing each Rasi into equal parts. Each divisional chart examines a specific area of life in detail.
Dosha
Literally means fault or blemish. Refers to specific planetary conditions that indicate areas of friction or challenge.
Drishti — Aspect
The influence one planet casts on another planet or house.
Exaltation — Uccha
The sign in which a planet's energy expresses most powerfully and clearly. Not a guaranteed blessing but a specific strength that carries its own responsibility.
Gochara
Transit. The current movement of planets through the zodiac and their relationship to the natal chart positions.
Graha
Planet. The nine Grahas of Jyotish are Surya, Chandra, Kuja, Budha, Guru, Shukra, Shani, Rahu, and Ketu.
Jyotish
Vedic astrology. From the Sanskrit root Jyoti meaning light. One of the six Vedangas — limbs of the Vedas.
Kaala Sarpa Dosha
A chart condition where all seven classical planets fall between Rahu and Ketu. Its interpretation varies significantly across different practitioners and traditions. Often overstated in popular astrology.
Karaka
Significator. Each planet naturally signifies specific areas of life.
Kendra
The four angular houses — first, fourth, seventh, and tenth. Considered the most powerful positions in the chart.
Kona
The three trine houses — first, fifth, and ninth. Considered the most auspicious houses in the chart.
Kundali
Birth chart. The complete map of planetary positions at the moment of birth calculated for the specific place of birth.
Lagna
Ascendant. The Rasi rising on the eastern horizon at the exact moment and place of birth.
Maha Dasha
The main planetary period in the Vimshottari Dasha system.
Mangal Dosha — Chevvai Dosham
A chart condition where Mars is placed in specific houses. Its effects are widely misunderstood and overstated in popular astrology. Classical texts list numerous conditions under which it is cancelled or reduced.
Mooltrikona
A specific degree range within a planet's own sign where it expresses with particular strength and clarity.
Muhurta
Auspicious timing. The selection of specific moments for important actions based on planetary positions and other Panchanga factors.
Nadi Dosha
A matching consideration where both partners share the same Nadi as determined by their birth Nakshatra. Its significance varies across traditions and must be read in context of the complete chart.
Nakshatra
Lunar mansion. The zodiac is divided into 27 Nakshatras each spanning 13 degrees and 20 minutes.
Navagraha
The nine planets of Jyotish — Sun, Moon, Mars, Mercury, Jupiter, Venus, Saturn, Rahu, and Ketu.
Navamsa — D9
The ninth divisional chart. Shows the soul's deeper nature, the quality of dharma and marriage, and the underlying strength of planets.
Panchanga
The five limbs of the Vedic calendar — Tithi, Vara, Nakshatra, Yoga, and Karana.
Parihara
Remedy. Specific practices, worship, charity, or lifestyle adjustments suggested to work consciously with challenging planetary conditions.
Prarabdha Karma
The portion of accumulated karma that has ripened and is being experienced in this specific life. The birth chart is the map of this Prarabdha.
Pratyantardasha
The sub sub period within an Antardasha.
Raja Yoga
A planetary combination indicating significant capacity for achievement, recognition, and influence.
Rasi
Sign. The twelve equal divisions of the zodiac each spanning 30 degrees.
Retrograde — Vakri
When a planet appears to move backward from Earth's perspective. Retrograde planets carry a more internalised, intensified quality of their energy.
Sarvashtakavarga
The combined Ashtakavarga table showing the total points across all planets for each house.
Shodashavarga
See Ashtadasha Varga. The sixteen divisional charts.
Tithi
Lunar day. One of the five limbs of the Panchanga.
Upachaya
The houses of growth — third, sixth, tenth, and eleventh. Malefic planets placed here tend to improve over time and with effort.
Vimshottari Dasha
The most widely used Dasha system in Jyotish. A 120 year cycle of nine planetary periods.
Yoga
A planetary combination. Specific configurations of planets that produce particular results. The presence of a Yoga indicates potential — its actual manifestation depends on the overall chart strength, Dasha periods, and conscious engagement of the person.
This glossary covers the most commonly encountered terms. Jyotish is a vast system with extensive terminology. If you encounter a term in your chart reading that is not listed here, the chart reading itself will explain its meaning in context.
