The 27 Nakshatras

The descriptions here are brief by intention. Detailed interpretation of your specific Nakshatra placement is given in your chart reading.
The Nakshatras are the 27 lunar mansions of Vedic astrology — each spanning 13 degrees and 20 minutes of the zodiac. Where the Rasis show the broad quality of a planetary placement, the Nakshatra shows its specific texture and depth. The Moon's Nakshatra at birth is particularly significant — it shows the most immediate quality of the soul's emotional and mental experience in this life.
1. Ashwini

Ruled by Ketu. Deity: Ashwini Kumaras. Symbol: Horse's head. Nature: Swift, initiating, healing. Its deepest quality is the capacity to arrive — to be fully present and ready without hesitation. Its learning is patience — that not everything genuine arrives as quickly as Ashwini moves.

2. Bharani

Ruled by Venus. Deity: Yama. Symbol: Yoni. Nature: Intense, transformative, bearing. Its deepest quality is the capacity to bear what is difficult without being destroyed by it. Its learning is release — that what has been carried long enough must eventually be let go.

3. Krittika

Ruled by the Sun. Deity: Agni. Symbol: Flame or razor. Nature: Sharp, purifying, cutting. Its deepest quality is discernment — the willingness to see and say what is true even when it is uncomfortable. Its learning is warmth — that the same fire that purifies can also nourish.

4. Rohini

Ruled by the Moon. Deity: Brahma. Symbol: Chariot or ox cart. Nature: Creative, fertile, grounded beauty. Its deepest quality is the capacity to bring things into beautiful, sustained existence. Its learning is non-attachment — that what is created does not belong to the creator.

5. Mrigashira

Ruled by Mars. Deity: Soma. Symbol: Deer's head. Nature: Seeking, sensitive, searching. Its deepest quality is genuine curiosity and sensitivity. Its learning is presence — that what is genuinely sought is often already here.

6. Ardra

Ruled by Rahu. Deity: Rudra. Symbol: Teardrop or diamond. Nature: Stormy, transformative, penetrating. Its deepest quality is the capacity for radical transformation through confronting what is raw and real. Its learning is stillness after the storm.

7. Punarvasu

Ruled by Jupiter. Deity: Aditi. Symbol: Quiver of arrows. Nature: Returning, renewing, expansive. Its deepest quality is genuine optimism rooted in understanding rather than denial. Its learning is groundedness.

8. Pushya

Ruled by Saturn. Deity: Brihaspati. Symbol: Flower or circle. Nature: Nourishing, stabilising, auspicious. Its deepest quality is the capacity to sustain and care for what genuinely matters. Its learning is receiving.

9. Ashlesha

Ruled by Mercury. Deity: Nagas. Symbol: Coiled serpent. Nature: Penetrating, intense, perceptive. Its deepest quality is penetrating intelligence and the capacity for genuine transformation. Its learning is trust.

10. Magha

Ruled by Ketu. Deity: Pitrs (ancestors). Symbol: Royal throne. Nature: Regal, ancestral, authoritative. Its deepest quality is the capacity for genuine leadership rooted in respect for tradition. Its learning is that true authority serves rather than dominates.

11. Purva Phalguni

Ruled by Venus. Deity: Bhaga. Symbol: Front legs of bed or hammock. Nature: Pleasurable, creative, restful. Its deepest quality is the capacity to participate fully in the beauty of existence. Its learning is that rest and pleasure are renewal for deeper engagement.

12. Uttara Phalguni

Ruled by the Sun. Deity: Aryaman. Symbol: Back legs of bed. Nature: Generous, sustaining, relational. Its deepest quality is loyalty rooted in understanding rather than obligation. Its learning is that genuine giving does not deplete.

13. Hasta

Ruled by the Moon. Deity: Savitar. Symbol: Hand. Nature: Skilled, practical, dexterous. Its deepest quality is craftsmanship — bringing something into existence with care and skill. Its learning is that the hands serve best when guided by genuine intention.

14. Chitra

Ruled by Mars. Deity: Vishwakarma. Symbol: Bright jewel. Nature: Creative, artistic, striking. Its deepest quality is the marriage of beauty and precision. Its learning is that genuine creation serves something beyond the creator's need for recognition.

15. Swati

Ruled by Rahu. Deity: Vayu. Symbol: Sword or coral. Nature: Independent, flexible, moving. Its deepest quality is genuine independence rooted in inner clarity. Its learning is rootedness.

16. Vishakha

Ruled by Jupiter. Deity: Indra and Agni. Symbol: Triumphal arch or potter's wheel. Nature: Purposeful, intense, goal oriented. Its deepest quality is genuine purposefulness. Its learning is that the goal must be examined honestly.

17. Anuradha

Ruled by Saturn. Deity: Mitra. Symbol: Lotus or staff. Nature: Devoted, disciplined, friendly. Its deepest quality is loyal, sustained care for what genuinely matters. Its learning is that devotion must be offered to what is genuinely worthy.

18. Jyeshtha

Ruled by Mercury. Deity: Indra. Symbol: Circular amulet or umbrella. Nature: Protective, eldest, responsible. Its deepest quality is genuine protective strength. Its learning is that carrying others' burdens without attending to one's own inner life eventually depletes rather than sustains.

19. Mula

Ruled by Ketu. Deity: Nirriti. Symbol: Tied roots or elephant goad. Nature: Rooting, dissolving, foundational. Its deepest quality is the courage to face what is most essential. Its learning is that destruction of the false must be followed by recognition of the real.

20. Purva Ashadha

Ruled by Venus. Deity: Apas (waters). Symbol: Fan or winnowing basket. Nature: Invincible, purifying, determined. Its deepest quality is genuine invincibility rooted in clarity of purpose. Its learning is humility.

21. Uttara Ashadha

Ruled by the Sun. Deity: Vishvadevas. Symbol: Elephant tusk or small bed. Nature: Universal, victorious, sustained. Its deepest quality is integrity. Its learning is that universal principles must be lived, not merely understood.

22. Shravana

Ruled by the Moon. Deity: Vishnu. Symbol: Ear or three footprints. Nature: Listening, learning, connecting. Its deepest quality is the wisdom that comes from genuine receptivity. Its learning is that true listening requires the willingness to be changed by what is heard.

23. Dhanishtha

Ruled by Mars. Deity: Eight Vasus. Symbol: Drum or flute. Nature: Abundant, rhythmic, musical. Its deepest quality is the recognition that genuine abundance is a natural state. Its learning is that the rhythm of life includes both fullness and emptiness.

24. Shatabhisha

Ruled by Rahu. Deity: Varuna. Symbol: Empty circle or hundred stars. Nature: Healing, mysterious, independent. Its deepest quality is genuine perceptive depth. Its learning is connection — that depth without the capacity to share what is seen becomes isolation.

25. Purva Bhadrapada

Ruled by Jupiter. Deity: Aja Ekapada. Symbol: Front legs of funeral cot or sword. Nature: Intense, transformative, two faced. Its deepest quality is the courage to transform completely. Its learning is that transformation in service of genuine growth is different from destruction for its own sake.

26. Uttara Bhadrapada

Ruled by Saturn. Deity: Ahir Budhnya. Symbol: Back legs of funeral cot or snake in water. Nature: Deep, wise, restrained. Its deepest quality is the stillness of deep water. Its learning is expression — that wisdom held entirely within eventually needs to find form.

27. Revati

Ruled by Mercury. Deity: Pushan. Symbol: Fish or drum. Nature: Nourishing, guiding, completing. Its deepest quality is genuine care for the journey of others. Its learning is that completion is not an ending — it is the threshold of a new beginning at a deeper level.

The Nakshatra of your Moon is the most immediately significant in your chart. The Nakshatras of your Lagna and other planets add further depth. No Nakshatra is superior or inferior to another. Their meaning in your specific chart is described in your chart reading.
