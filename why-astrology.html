Why Astrology

Why Astrology

Most people come to astrology looking for answers about what will happen. Will this relationship work. Will this career succeed. When will things get better. These are natural human questions. But they rest on an assumption — that life is something happening to us, and we are trying to navigate it from the outside.

Jyotish begins from a different place entirely.

If the soul is a unit of consciousness on a journey — carrying specific karma from its own past, moving through specific conditions in this life, with a direction that points toward liberation — then the question is not what will happen. The question is who am I, what am I carrying, and what is this life asking me to work through.

Knowing your Prarabdha matters because it replaces confusion with clarity. Not the false clarity of "this will happen on this date" — but the genuine clarity of understanding your own nature, your own patterns, and the specific friction your soul has chosen for this life.

Without this clarity, a person experiences their karma as random — good luck and bad luck, reward and punishment, unfair burdens and unearned gifts. They spend their energy reacting. Resisting what is difficult. Clinging to what is comfortable. Neither reaction moves them forward. Both keep them in the same place.

With this clarity, the same experiences are seen differently. A persistent difficulty is not a sign of being cursed or unlucky. It is a specific condition the soul is working through. A recurring pattern in relationships, in work, in health — is not random misfortune. It is the same karma presenting itself again, asking to be understood rather than escaped.

This shift — from reacting to understanding — is the beginning of conscious participation in one's own life. And conscious participation is the beginning of sadhana.

Astrology does not give you liberation. No external system can. What it gives you is an honest map of where you are — what you are carrying, what you are avoiding, what you are ready for. What you do with that map is entirely your own work.

This is why astrology matters. Not because it predicts the future. But because it illuminates the present with enough honesty that a person can stop being moved only by circumstances and begin moving by understanding.

That movement — however small, however gradual — is the direction of liberation.

What Astrology Can Tell You

  • The nature and tendencies you were born with
  • The specific karmic patterns active in this life
  • The quality and direction of different periods of your life
  • The areas of life that carry the most friction for your growth
  • The natural strengths available to you
  • The timing of significant shifts in life conditions

What Astrology Cannot Tell You

  • Exactly what will happen and when — the chart shows tendencies, not certainties
  • What choices you will make — free will operates within karma, not outside it
  • Whether you will pursue liberation or ignore it — that is always your choice
  • The precise details of events — a chart shows the nature of a period, not its exact contents
  • Anything with certainty if the birth time is inaccurate — the chart is only as reliable as the data it is built on
